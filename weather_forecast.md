## Key Crew Takeaways

### ✅ Thunderstorm Completely Gone
The most important update: **no thunderstorm code anywhere in the forecast**. The MID zone shows only light drizzle (code 53, 0.7 mm/hr) at 5 PM on descent. The entire T-storm threat that dominated the past week of forecasts has dissolved. Dry Fork (3 PM) and Upper Sheep Creek (4:30 PM) are clear and dry. The primary weather risks have completely shifted.

### 🌡️ HEAT — New Primary Concern (CRITICAL CHANGE)
Valley temps have jumped significantly from the June 18 forecast. **Sally's Footbridge at 11 AM is now 75°F** (up from 68°F), and the **valley peaks at 84°F at 3 PM** (up from 78°F). This is 6°F hotter than yesterday's model:

- Runners at Sally's (Mi 18) will face their first real heat exposure — **sunscreen and aggressive hydration here is critical**
- The valley ascent back to Bear Camp (Mi 21.5) climbs from 75°F valley floor into 68°F MID zone — watch for runners who under-hydrated at Sally's
- Crew at Dry Fork should prioritize **cold drinks and electrolytes** — runners arriving at 1–3 PM have been in warm MID conditions all afternoon
- **Heat cramps and GI issues more likely** than in any previous model run

### 💨 HIGH Zone Wind at Start — First 3 Hours (NEW HAZARD)
The start ridge is significantly windier than any prior forecast. **Jaws Trailhead at 5 AM: 9 mph sustained, 17 mph gusts.** Worsens through 7 AM (13 mph / 21 mph gusts) before dropping as runners descend off the ridge by 8 AM.

- Wind + 44°F = windchill ~35°F — feels much colder than the raw temperature
- Base layer, gloves, and wind shell are essential for the first 2–3 hours even if temperatures look mild
- Wind drops dramatically once runners reach Spring Marsh (Mi 8.5, 8 AM) — but they need to survive the ridge first

### 💨 MID Zone Wind — Plateau Climb (12–1 PM)
After the ridge, wind returns on the MID plateau. **Bear Camp climb through Kearn's Cow Camp shows 13–15 mph sustained, 18–19 mph gusts at 12–1 PM.** Exposed trail on plateau. Wind eases by 3 PM at Dry Fork (7 mph).

### ⚠️ Valley Gust Spike — 5 PM Still Present
The 25 mph gust pattern in the valley persists. **VAL 17:00 shows 24.8 mph gusts** with 80°F temps and 16 mph sustained wind. This arrives during the descent into the valley floor, just before runners reach Tongue River TH. **Keep all canopies anchored at Tongue River TH setup.** Gusts ease to 19 mph by 6 PM and 13 mph by 7 PM.

### 🏁 Warm Dry Finish
**Scott Park at 8 PM: 71°F, overcast, dry, nearly calm (3 mph / 5 mph gusts).** A comfortable finish window for late runners. 53% probability is elevated but no precipitation is forecast. Late finishers will NOT be running through rain.
