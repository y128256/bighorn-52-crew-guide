## Key Crew Takeaways

### ⚡ Thunderstorm Now at Dry Fork — 3 Hours Earlier (CRITICAL CHANGE)
The biggest shift from June 16: the thunderstorm window has moved **3 hours earlier**. The MID zone now shows **weathercode 95 (thunderstorm) at 2–3 PM** with 1.1–1.3 mm/hr precipitation. This puts the storm directly at the Dry Fork cutoff (Mi 34.5, cutoff 3:00 PM) — the crew car stop.

- Runners will arrive at Dry Fork in an active thunderstorm
- Crew at Dry Fork must be prepared for lightning shelter, wet/cold runners, and quick gear swap
- Runners who left Upper Sheep Creek (Mi 39.5) at 4:30 PM cutoff will be in post-storm drizzle (code 53, ~55°F)
- **This is now a crew execution window, not a weather-watch window — plan for storm gear hand-off at Dry Fork**

### ☁️ Valley Overcast All Day — Clear Breaks Gone
Unlike the June 16 forecast which showed clear breaks at 11 AM and 3 PM, the valley is **overcast all day (code 3)**. Sally's Footbridge (Mi 18) will be warm but not sunny — ~72°F. The valley peak drops slightly to **78°F at 3–5 PM** (was 83°F). Hydration management still critical at Sally's through Dry Fork climb, but less solar heat load than feared.

### 🌧️ Late MID Zone Heavy Rain — 6–7 PM
After the 2–3 PM storm, the MID zone does not dry out. **Code 63 (heavy rain, 6.6 mm/hr) at 7 PM** and showers (code 80, 2.2 mm/hr) at 6 PM. Runners who miss Dry Fork cutoff and are still in the MID zone will face severe late-evening rain. This also means trails near Tongue River TH may be waterlogged from runoff.

### 🌧️ Wet Finish — Showers at 8 PM
The valley finally receives rain at race end: **showers (code 80) at 8 PM with 1.4 mm** at Scott Park. Runners finishing on the cutoff wire will cross the line in the rain. Crew at Scott Park should have cover and dry clothes staged for the finish.

### 💨 Gust Spike at Home Stretch (7 PM)
VALLEY 7 PM shows a sharp gust spike to **16.3 mph** with nearly calm sustained wind (1.8 mph). This is sudden and localized. Check tent/canopy anchoring at Tongue River TH before the 6:45 PM cutoff window, as the gust front arrives shortly after.

### ❄️ Cold Exposure — Storm Descent (3–5 PM)
MID zone temps fall sharply through the storm window: 58°F at 3 PM (Dry Fork), 55°F at 4 PM (Upper Sheep Creek), then descending through drizzle at 5 PM. Runners near the 3 PM cutoff are at elevated hypothermia risk — cold, wet, post-storm, and still have 17+ miles to go. **Rain shell + insulation layer should be in Dry Fork drop bag or handed off by crew.**
