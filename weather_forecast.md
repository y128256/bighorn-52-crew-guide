## Key Crew Takeaways

### ✅ No Thunderstorm — Still Confirmed
No thunderstorm code in any zone at any hour. Dry Fork (3 PM) and Upper Sheep Creek (4:30 PM) are clear. This is stable across both June 19 model runs.

### ⚠️ Gust Spike Moves to 7 PM — 30 mph at Home Stretch (CRITICAL CHANGE)
The gust hazard has **shifted from 5–6 PM to 7 PM** and intensified significantly. VAL 19:00 shows **22 mph sustained, 30 mph gusts** — the strongest valley wind in any forecast this week. This hits exactly at the **Home Stretch (Mi 49.7) and finish approach window**:

- Any canopy, tent, or gear at Scott Park or Tongue River TH must be anchored **before** runners arrive
- The finish line itself (8 PM) still sees 20 mph gusts — the front doesn't fully clear until after 8 PM
- The 6:45 PM Tongue River TH cutoff window (VAL 18:00) is 19 mph gusts — manageable, but rising fast
- The gust front clears quickly: by 9 PM valley drops to 11 mph

### 🌡️ Heat Still Real — Valley Peak 82°F
Valley peaks at **82°F at 3 PM** (down slightly from morning's 84°F). Sally's Footbridge (11 AM) is now **67°F** — meaningfully cooler than the 75°F in the morning model, so the heat window at Sally's is reduced. Primary heat exposure is now the mid-afternoon valley and the Dry Fork approach climb.

- Crew at Dry Fork: cold drinks and electrolytes remain essential — runners have been in warm conditions since noon
- **82°F is still hot** — do not skip sunscreen or hydration just because the number dropped

### 💨 Wind Persists Longer on the Ridge
The HIGH zone stays gusty further into the morning than the earlier model showed. **Gusts reach 24 mph at 10 AM** on the ridge (Spring Marsh / Cathedral Rock elevation). The MID zone also shows 22 mph gusts at 8 AM. Runners who leave the ridge early (fast starters) clear the worst of it; mid-pack runners still experience significant wind through the first 2–3 hours.

- Wind shell and gloves remain critical for the first ~3 hours
- 44°F + 17 mph = windchill ~33°F at start

### 🏁 Finish Gusty but Dry
Unlike the calm finish in the morning forecast, **8 PM now shows 20 mph gusts** at Scott Park. Runners crossing late will face wind. Still **completely dry** — no rain in the valley at any point.
