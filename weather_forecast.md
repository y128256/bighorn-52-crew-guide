## Key Crew Takeaways

### ✅ Thunderstorm Delayed — Dry Fork Now Clear (CRITICAL CHANGE)

- **Dry Fork (Mi 34.5, cutoff 3:00 PM) is now just overcast** — 69% precip probability but no active storm, no lightning risk at the cutoff station

- Crew at Dry Fork should still stage rain gear for the approaching storm, but the worst timing threat is gone
- Runners departing Dry Fork post-cutoff still face the full T-storm

### ⚠️ New: Major Gust Spike at Tongue River TH — 6 PM (NEW HAZARD)
VALLEY 18:00 shows **25.1 mph gusts** (was 11 mph in June 17). This is a sharp gust front arriving at the crew position during the **6:45 PM cutoff window**:

- Any canopy, tent, or gear set up at Tongue River TH is at risk
- Anchor everything heavily before arriving — the spike is sudden
- The gust front eases by 7 PM (12 mph) and 8 PM (9 mph)

### ✅ Finish Line is DRY
Unlike June 17 which showed showers at 8 PM (code 80), the valley finish at Scott Park is **overcast but dry at the cutoff (67°F)**. Late finishers crossing the line won't face active rain. However, dense drizzle (code 55, 1.1 mm) arrives at 9 PM — just after the cutoff.

The severe late MID rain from June 17 (code 63, 6.6 mm/hr at 7 PM) has been replaced by **moderate drizzle (code 53, 0.5 mm/hr)**. Runners still in the MID zone after the cutoff face wet conditions but not heavy rain. Trail runoff toward Tongue River TH should be less severe than feared.

VALLEY 17:00 shows a sharp temperature drop (77°F → 64°F in one hour) with **16 mph sustained wind** and 13 mph gusts — this is the leading edge of the storm system hitting the valley floor. Followed immediately by the 25 mph gust spike at 18:00. The crew window at Tongue River TH overlaps directly with this frontal passage.

### ❄️ Cold Exposure — Storm at 4 PM (Shifted Risk Window)
The T-storm now peaks at 4 PM during the Upper Sheep Creek cutoff. Runners leaving on the 4:30 PM cutoff will be in active thunderstorm conditions (~60°F, wet, exposed). The Dry Fork → Upper Sheep Creek segment (Mi 34.5–39.5) is the coldest and most exposed stretch. **Rain shell + insulation still critical at Dry Fork drop bag — timing of risk window shifted 1 hour later, not eliminated.**
