## Key Crew Takeaways

### ⚡ Thunderstorm Risk — 5–6 PM MID Zone (NEW — Major Change)
The biggest change from June 14: the MID zone now shows **weathercode 95 (thunderstorm) at 5–6 PM** with 1.1–1.7 mm/hr precipitation. Runners who start their descent from Upper Sheep Creek / Mi 39.5 at 4:30 PM cutoff will be in this window. This is exposed terrain — no tree cover above treeline. Hazard:

- Lightning risk on ridgeline descent (Mi 39.5–44)
- 59°F + rain + wind on descent = significant wind chill
- This is the highest-risk weather window of the race

Crew should be prepared for runners arriving at Tongue River TH (Mi 46.7) wet, cold, and potentially shaken.

### ☀️ Clear Valley Breaks — Morning and Afternoon
Unlike the all-day overcast of the June 14 forecast, the valley (Dayton area) now shows **clear sky breaks at 11 AM and 3 PM**. Sally's Footbridge (Mi 18, 11 AM) will be warmer and sunnier than expected. Then overcast returns as storm builds.

### 🌡️ Cooler Start + Hotter Valley Peak
- Start at Jaws: **48°F** (was 51°F — back to typical alpine cool start)
- Valley peak: **83°F at 4 PM** — hottest forecast yet
- The valley heats up fast after the 11 AM clear break; hydration management critical at Sally's through Dry Fork climb

### 🌧️ Dry Morning — Then Fast-Building Afternoon Storm
Previous forecast had drizzle starting at noon. This forecast shows **no precipitation until 5 PM**, then the storm hits hard (1.1–1.7 mm/hr with thunder). Probability escalates to 78% from 1 PM onward but actual precip is concentrated at 5–6 PM. The dry afternoon may lull runners into not carrying rain gear — **runners need their shell at Dry Fork (Mi 34.5) regardless.**

### 💨 Valley Evening Gusts — Reduced
Good news: evening gusts in the valley have dropped from the alarming 22–24 mph in the June 14 forecast to **10–13 mph** at 6–8 PM. Still worth staking tents at Tongue River TH and finish, but the gust threat is much less severe.

> ⚠️ **Note on VALLEY 5 PM wind reading (30.4 mph sustained / 6.3 mph gusts):** This is almost certainly a model artifact — sustained wind higher than gusts is physically inconsistent. Treat the 5 PM valley wind as unreliable; the 6 PM reading (11 mph sustained / 13 mph gusts) is more plausible.

### ❄️ Cold Exposure — Late-Day Descent
MID zone temps drop from 69°F at 3 PM to 59°F at 5 PM, coinciding with the thunderstorm. Runners near the cutoff will be cold, wet, and tired in this window — the highest hypothermia risk of the race.
